Extended Final Analysis

Most Preferred Vacancy — Physics Teacher in Kazakhstan — 90% compatibility

This vacancy is currently the most suitable and realistic career option for me. My academic background in Physics and Physics Education directly matches the employer's expectations. I already possess pedagogical training, classroom experience, and multilingual communication skills (Kazakh, Russian, English), which are highly valued in Kazakhstan's education system.

The demand for qualified physics teachers in specialized schools is consistently high, especially in STEM-focused institutions. This guarantees stable employment and long-term career security. In addition, the salary level is competitive relative to the local cost of living, and there are opportunities for rapid professional growth — such as becoming a senior teacher, curriculum developer, or academic coordinator.

Another important advantage is that this position allows me to immediately apply my specialization without requiring additional certification or relocation barriers. I can begin working right after graduation while continuing professional development.

From a strategic perspective, this role can act as a foundation for future international work. Teaching experience gained in Kazakhstan can later support applications to international schools or graduate research programs abroad.

Why I would choose this position:

  • Maximum alignment with my degree and skills
  • Fast employment without extra licensing
  • Strong job stability
  • Growing demand for STEM educators
  • Competitive salary in the local market
  • Opportunity to build teaching portfolio
  • Career advancement inside the education sector
  • Possibility to transition later into international education

This vacancy represents the best balance between realism, income, and long-term career planning.

Least Suitable Vacancy — University Lecturer in CIS — 72% compatibility

Reason: Insufficient scientific publications.

This position is currently the least accessible due to insufficient academic research background. Universities in CIS countries prioritize candidates with scientific publications, conference participation, and active research portfolios. While my education and teaching ability meet basic requirements, my research profile is not yet competitive enough.

The main barrier is the lack of peer-reviewed publications and research projects. Academic institutions evaluate candidates not only as teachers but also as contributors to scientific knowledge. Without a strong publication record, the probability of employment remains limited.

However, this vacancy is not impossible — it simply requires structured preparation.

Improvement strategy:

Year 1:

  • Write and submit 2 research papers
  • Participate in national or international conferences
  • Collaborate with university professors on research

Year 2:

  • Join a funded research project
  • Publish additional articles
  • Build an academic CV and research portfolio

Estimated preparation period: 1–2 years

After completing this plan, compatibility may increase from 72% to approximately 90-95%. This path is more suitable as a long-term academic goal rather than an immediate employment option.

Conclusion

At the present stage of my professional development, the optimal strategy is to begin working as a physics teacher in Kazakhstan. This position offers immediate employment, strong compatibility with my qualifications, and a stable financial foundation.

This dual strategy allows both short-term employment success and long-term global mobility. The Kazakhstan teaching position serves as a practical launch platform, while research and certification efforts open future access to international schools and university careers.
